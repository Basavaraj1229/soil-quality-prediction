🌍 What is Soil and Why It Matters?

Soil is the thin layer of material that covers the earth’s surface and is a key natural resource that sustains life on Earth. It provides nutrients to plants, stores water, and supports biodiversity. Good quality soil helps ensure better crop yield and environmental health.

  • Anchors plant roots and supplies nutrients.
  • Filters water and breaks down organic matter.
  • Supports billions of organisms essential for plant growth.

🛠️ How to Improve Soil Quality

  • Add organic compost and manure to enrich nutrients.
  • Test and balance pH using lime or sulfur if needed.
  • Reduce chemical fertilizers; use bio-fertilizers.
  • Implement crop rotation and cover crops.
  • Ensure good drainage to prevent waterlogging.

🦠 Common Soil-Related Diseases

  • Fusarium Wilt: Yellowing and drooping caused by fungus in soil.
  • Root Rot: Excess moisture leading to decay of roots.
  • Nematodes: Microscopic worms that damage plant roots.
  • Powdery Mildew: Fungal disease affecting surface and roots.
  • Clubroot: Swelling and rotting of roots due to soilborne pathogen.
